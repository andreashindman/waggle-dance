Honey Bee Communication: Waggle Dance

 Honey bees communicate learned information about the nearest food source to the rest of the hive by performing the waggle dance. Upon returning to the hive after finding a lucrative source of food or water, the honey bee will perform this dance by walking in a figure '8' shape and doing a waggle in the middle. For distances less than 40 meters, variations of the waggle dance are performed. For distances greater than 40 meters, the time it takes for the bee to complete a circuit indicates the distance to the source; The orientation of the dance in relation to the hive indicates the direction of the source relative to the sun; and the magnitude of the waggling indicates how exciting the food source is.

 The angle between the verticle of the sun and the orientation of the waggle phase relative the the hive encode the direction of the food source relative to the sun.
